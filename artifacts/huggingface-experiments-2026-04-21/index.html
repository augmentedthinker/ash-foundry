Experiment 01 · First successful image generation

This first stored Hugging Face experiment was generated immediately after validating the local token. The goal was simple: prove that Hugging Face can return a real image artifact from this machine, not just account metadata.

Prompt: “A cinematic portrait of a forge spirit made of ember light and brass machinery, standing in a dark workshop, volumetric light, highly detailed, painterly realism.”

Outcome: success. A real image file was returned and archived in the Foundry assets folder.

Observed visual result: the output leaned toward a rugged blacksmith / artisan portrait rather than a more abstract machine-spirit figure, but the image quality itself was strong enough to count as a meaningful route worth continuing to test.

Why this matters

This experiment establishes Hugging Face as a real creative recovery lane inside the Foundry. Google free-tier image generation is currently blocked by quota. Pollinations works, but its quality is less dependable. Hugging Face now sits in the middle: more setup than Pollinations, but with much stronger potential for higher-quality image outputs.

The next step should be structured comparison testing across at least three Hugging Face image routes, then deciding whether this becomes the primary image path or only one lane in a broader multi-provider stack.

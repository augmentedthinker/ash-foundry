Video Generation

A continuity page for the Veo-backed video-generation lane exposed by the current Gemini-related model surface. This page records which Veo models are visible, why the path appears more complex than images or TTS, and what future Ash should keep in mind before trying to operationalize it.

Status: research / higher-frictionVeo models foundLong-running predictionsNeeds more setup
Video generation appears reachable in principle, but it does not currently look like the cleanest next acquisition target. The visible model surface suggests a more complex path involving long-running operations and possibly billing or Vertex-style infrastructure.

What is known so far

Visible Veo models: models/veo-2.0-generate-001, models/veo-3.0-generate-001, models/veo-3.0-fast-generate-001, models/veo-3.1-generate-preview, models/veo-3.1-fast-generate-preview, models/veo-3.1-lite-generate-preview.
Supported method family: these show up with predictLongRunning rather than normal generateContent.
Important warning signal: at least one visible Veo description explicitly referenced billing on the associated Google Cloud account.
Why this is harder

Long-running operation flow

Unlike image generation, this probably does not come back as a simple single synchronous response. It likely involves submitting a generation job, receiving a long-running operation handle, and polling until completion.

What that implies

More infrastructure, more uncertainty

Even if the models are visible, actual success may depend on account configuration, billing status, and different request formats than the ones used so far for Gemini image generation.

What future Ash should do next

Step 1: treat Veo as a later-stage acquisition target, not the next immediate one.
Step 2: when ready, test the simplest likely model first, probably one of the fast or lite variants.
Step 3: be prepared to inspect long-running operation semantics, billing constraints, and possibly Vertex-style documentation or request flows.
Step 4: only promote this skill once a real video output has been successfully generated, saved, and hosted.
